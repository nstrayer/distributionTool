Normal Distribution

Form:

$$p(x) = \frac{1}{\sigma \sqrt{2 \pi}} e^{-(x-\mu)^2 / 2 \sigma^2}$$

$\mu$ represents the mean of the distribution and $\sigma$ the standard deviation.

Standard Normal:

If you set $\mu = 0$ and $\sigma = 1$ you arrive at what is known as the standard normal.

$$p(x) = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2 \pi}} e^{-x^2 / 2 }$$

This form is useful as you can remember rules for one distribution and apply them to others by converting to standard normal with the tranformation $z = (x- \mu)/\sigma$.
